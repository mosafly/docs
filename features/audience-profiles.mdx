Audience profiles are AI-generated personas that represent your target segments. Each profile gives the AI a specific person to design for — their identity, what holds them back from buying, and the message angle most likely to resonate. When you use profiles in a campaign, each one produces its own set of visuals tailored to that audience.

## What a profile contains

| Field | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Persona** | Who this person is | "Woman, 30–45, urban, values authenticity, higher income" |
| **Pain point** | Their main barrier or fear | "Afraid of giving an impersonal or disappointing gift" |
| **Angle** | The message strategy to use | "'Craftsmanship as proof of value' — lead with artisanal know-how" |

The AI uses all three fields to adapt the composition, dominant colors, and generated text for every creative tied to that profile.

## Generating profiles

The fastest way to build your profile library is to let the AI generate ten profiles from a brand description.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Brand Setup">
    In the sidebar, go to **Brand Setup → Profiles**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate profiles">
    Click **Generate profiles**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe your brand">
    Enter a description of your brand in the text field. The richer the description, the more accurate and differentiated the profiles will be.

    **Example of an effective brand description:**

    ```text
    French artisan chocolate brand, premium positioning, gift market,
    targeting 30–55 year-olds looking for quality gifts for special
    occasions. Differentiators: bean-to-bar process, unique origins,
    Parisian workshops.
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select output language">
    Choose the language in which you want the profiles written, then confirm.

    The AI generates **10 distinct profiles** in **30–60 seconds**. They are immediately available to use in your campaigns.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Choosing the right profiles

After generation, read through all ten profiles and identify the two or three most relevant to your next campaign. Look for:

- A **persona** that matches your primary target audience
- A **pain point** that reflects a real barrier to purchase
- An **angle** that aligns with your current commercial message

## Using profiles in campaigns

In the campaign creation wizard (Step 2 — Targeting), select up to 12 profiles. Each selected profile generates its own creatives, giving you multiple creative angles from a single batch run. See [Campaigns](/features/campaigns) for the full workflow.

## Managing your profiles

Profiles belong to the active workspace. From the profiles section you can:

- View the full detail of any profile (persona, pain point, angle)
- Filter your existing campaign creatives by profile
- Add profiles manually if you already have defined personas

<Note>
  Profiles are shared across all campaigns in the same workspace. If your targeting strategy evolves — new segments, new market, seasonal shift — regenerate your profiles to keep them current.
</Note>
